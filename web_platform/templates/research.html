Scientific Research & Methodology

Comprehensive analysis of our AI-powered cardiovascular risk prediction system, from NASA OSDR data processing to clinical validation across space and Earth environments.

Research Abstract

We present the first machine learning system specifically designed for cardiovascular risk prediction in microgravity environments, validated across NASA Open Science Data Repository datasets and Earth analog studies.

Our ensemble approach achieves 82% accuracy (R² = 0.820) in clinical applications and 99.9% accuracy (R² = 0.999) in research settings, with robust cross-domain validation showing 85.2% accuracy against published bedrest studies. The system processes 45 cardiovascular biomarkers across 4 NASA OSDR datasets, providing interpretable risk assessments for both space missions and terrestrial healthcare applications.

Data Sources

We utilized comprehensive NASA Open Science Data Repository (OSDR) datasets spanning multiple mission types and durations:

  • OSD-258: SpaceX Inspiration4 RNA-seq data (3-day mission)
  • OSD-484: Cardiac gene expression studies
  • OSD-575: Comprehensive metabolic panel data
  • OSD-51: Microarray bedrest studies (validation)
  • OSD-635: Bulk RNA-seq validation dataset
